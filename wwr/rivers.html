The Qingshui Cliff

The Qingshui Cliffs are a series of towering cliffs located on the eastern coast of Taiwan. These cliffs stretch for eight kilometers along the Pacific Ocean, with some sections rising up to 800 meters above sea level. The Qingshui Cliffs are a popular tourist destination, offering breathtaking views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the area on foot or by car, and there are several lookout points that offer panoramic views of the cliffs and the ocean. In addition to their scenic beauty, the Qingshui Cliffs are also an important geological site. The cliffs were formed over millions of years by the collision of the Eurasian and Philippine tectonic plates, and they contain a wealth of geological information. Overall, the Qingshui Cliffs are a must-see destination for anyone visiting Taiwan, offering a stunning natural landscape and a glimpse into the island's rich geological history.
